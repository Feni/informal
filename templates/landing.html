A new medium of thought

“A computer language is not just a way of getting a computer to perform operations but rather that it is a novel formal medium for expressing ideas about methodology. Thus, programs must be written for people to read, and only incidentally for machines to execute.”
- Structure and Interpretation of Computer Programs

Like spoken languages, programming languages are a medium of thought. The expressive capabilities of the language you're working in shapes the way you approach a problem. The coding primitives we traditionally work with are designed around how code is parsed and executed. Arevel is focused on expressing ideas, solving problems and enabling creativity. With the right tools, we can expand the capabilities of the human mind and solve ever more complex problems of the world. We believe the right tools can expand the capabilities of the human mind.
